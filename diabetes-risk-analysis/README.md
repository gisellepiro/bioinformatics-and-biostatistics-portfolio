# Exploratory Data Analysis and Predictive Modeling of Type 2 Diabetes Risk

## Overview

This project investigates clinical and anthropometric factors associated with Type 2 Diabetes using a publicly available clinical dataset from rural Virginia, USA.

The analysis combines exploratory data analysis, statistical hypothesis testing, feature engineering and predictive modelling to identify variables associated with diabetes risk and glycated haemoglobin levels (HbA1c).

## Dataset

The study includes clinical information from 403 African American participants evaluated for diabetes and cardiovascular risk factors.

Variables include:

* Age
* Blood pressure
* Body mass index (BMI)
* Waist-to-hip ratio
* Cholesterol and HDL cholesterol
* Glycated haemoglobin (HbA1c)
* Fasting blood glucose

## Objectives

* Explore the prevalence of Type 2 Diabetes within the study population.
* Evaluate the relationship between obesity-related variables and diabetes.
* Investigate associations between blood pressure, age, lipid profile and HbA1c levels.
* Develop predictive models for diabetes risk estimation.

## Methods

* Data cleaning and preprocessing
* Feature engineering
* Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
* Correlation analysis
* Simple linear regression
* Multiple linear regression
* Probability calculations
* Monte Carlo simulation

## Main Findings

* Diabetes prevalence in the study population was approximately 15%.
* Age, cholesterol-to-HDL ratio and fasting blood glucose were the strongest predictors of HbA1c levels.
* Individual predictors showed limited explanatory power, highlighting the multifactorial nature of Type 2 Diabetes.
* The best predictive model explained approximately 59% of the variability in HbA1c.
